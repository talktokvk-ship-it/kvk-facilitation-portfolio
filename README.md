Facilitation & Gamification Portfolio 🎯

A portfolio site documenting 13 years of enterprise facilitation, retrospective design, and large-scale planning work — built to show the technique, not just describe it.

🔗 Live site: kvk-facilitation-portfolio.netlify.app

What this is

Most coaching portfolios describe capabilities in bullet points. This one is built around evidence: named formats, real outcomes, and one retrospective — the "Jeep Retro" — walked through in full depth, from the intervention itself to what shipped afterward and what didn't.

The core idea running through the site: a team fixes what it can see, and learns fastest by playing, not being told. Every section — the story mapping case study, the format library, the games — is evidence for that one coaching philosophy, not a disconnected list of skills.

What's on the site
The System — five mechanisms (whole-product visualization, visible delivery boards, a 30+ format retrospective library, a rotating engagement cadence, and purpose-built training games), each tied to a cadence and an outcome, not just a description
Story Mapping case study — a detailed walkthrough of running User Story Mapping and Big Room Planning across 10+ teams on a single product, including how one backlog card became a five-team, four-iteration delivery plan
Evidence Wall — a sample of retrospective formats (Weather Map, Team Health Radar, Sailboat, Retro Dart, Value/Effort Quadrant, the Ball Point Game, and more), each with the specific outcome it produced for a real team
The Jeep Retro — a full deep-dive on a single retrospective at redBus: a payment outage, a four-minute video with no answer given, and how the team self-diagnosed a monolith architecture problem that led to a targeted microservices migration — including an honest account of what didn't get fixed and why
Design notes

Built as a single-file site with a kraft/paper visual language — cream backgrounds, sienna and moss-green accents — distinct from the navy/teal system used on the companion Agile Coaching Portfolio, since this one is about tactile, in-the-room facilitation rather than structured delivery process.

How it's built
Single-page site, no framework, no build step
Built through conversational prompting — I don't have a traditional engineering background; this was AI-assisted design and content work, structured around real facilitation case studies
Deployed on Netlify
